Animal bite 動物咬傷
▽ <415660> △ <2015-12-01> § <TOC|KW> ☆
[[(Fig.)USN Medic Louis Kost gets attacked by Bruno crop retouch]]
An animal bite is a wound received from the teeth of an animal, including humans. Animals may bite in self-defence, in an attempt to prey on food, and as part of normal interactions. Other bite attacks may be apparently unprovoked. Self-inflicted bites occur in some genetic illnesses, such as Lesch-Nyhan syndrome. Biting is an act that occurs when an animal uses its teeth to pierce another object, including food, flesh, and inanimate matter. A person bitten by an animal potentially carrying parvovirus or rabies virus should consult a physician immediately. Those who have been bitten by an animal may also develop bacterial infections of the bone called osteomyelitis which can become life-threatening if untreated, whether or not the animal has parvovirus or rabies virus.

■Signs and symptoms
Bite wounds can cause a number of signs and symptoms

・Generalized tissue damage due to tearing and scratching
・Serious hemorrhage if major blood vessels are pierced
・Infection by bacteria or other pathogens, including rabies
・Introduction of venom into the wound by venomous animals such as some snakes
・Introduction of other irritants into the wound, causing inflammation and itching

■Causes
[[(Fig.)A mosquito bite]]
Bites are usually classified by the type of creature causing the wound.Many different creatures are known to bite humans.

□Vertebrates
・Dog bites are commonplace, with children the most commonly bitten and the face the most common target.([1]web: Dog Bite Statistics http://www.dogbitelaw.com/PAGES/statistics.html ) About 4.7 million dog bites are reported annually in the United States.([2][Questions and Answers about Dog Bites http://www.hsus.org/pets/pet_care/dog_care/stay_dog_bite_free/questions_and_answers_about_dog_bites/ ][リンク切れ])
・Other companion animals, including cats, ferrets, and parrots, may bite humans.
・Wildlife may sometimes bite humans. The bites of various mammals such as bats, skunks, wolves, raccoons, etc. may transmit rabies, which is almost always fatal if left untreated.[citation needed]
Involuntary biting injuries due to closed-fist injuries from fists striking teeth (referred to as reverse bite injuries) are a common consequence of fist fights. These have been termed "fight bites". Injuries in which the knuckle joints or tendons of the hand are bitten into tend to be the most serious.
Teething infants are known to bite objects to relieve pressure on their growing teeth, and may inadvertently bite people's hands or arms while doing so. Young children may also bite people out of anger or misbehaviour, although this is usually corrected early in the child's life.
In spite of their name, love bites are not biting injuries (they involve bruising from sucking, and the skin is not broken), although actual biting injuries are sometimes seen as the result of fetishistic activities.

■Treatment
Initial treatment includes washing the bite wound.([3]web: Animal bites Fact sheet N°373 http://www.who.int/mediacentre/factsheets/fs373/en/ ) If there is a low risk of infection the wound may be sutured.(ib. WHO2013)

□Antibiotics
Antibiotics to prevent infection are recommended for dog and cat bites of the hand,([4]web: BestBets: Antibiotics in cat bites http://www.bestbets.org/bets/bet.php?id=1021 ) and human bites if they are more than superficial.([5]Oehler RL, Velez AP, Mizrachi M, Lamarche J, Gompf S「Bite-related and septic syndrome caused by cats and dogs」『Lancet Infect Dis』第9巻第7号、2009年。439-47頁。DOI[ http://dx.doi.org/10.1016/S1473-3099(09)70110-0 ]) They are also recommended in those who have poor immune function.(ib. WHO2013) Evidence for antibiotics to prevent infection in bites in other areas is not clear.([6]Medeiros I, Saconato H「Antibiotic prophylaxis for mammalian bites」『Cochrane Database Syst Rev』第2号、2001年。CD001738頁。DOI[ http://dx.doi.org/10.1002/14651858.CD001738 ])
The first choice is amoxicillin with clavulanic acid, and if the person is penicillin-allergic, doxycycline and metronidazole.(ib. "Oehler2009") The antistaphylococcal penicillins (e.g., cloxacillin, nafcillin, flucloxacillin) and the macrolides (e.g., erythromycin, clarithromycin) are not used for empirical therapy, because they do not cover Pasteurella species.(ib. "Oehler2009")

□Rabies
Animal bites, most commonly dogs and bats, transmit rabies to humans.([7]web: Rabies Fact Sheet N°99 http://www.who.int/mediacentre/factsheets/fs099/en/ ) Rabies from other animals is rare.(ib. WHO2015) If the animal is caught alive or dead with its head preserved, the head can be analyzed to detect the disease. Signs of rabies include foaming at the mouth, self-mutilation, growling, jerky behavior, and red eyes.
If the animal cannot be captured, preventative rabies treatment is recommended in many places. Certain places, such as Hawaii, Australia, and the United Kingdom, are known not to have native rabies. Treatment is generally available in North America and the Northern European states.

□Tetanus
Tetanus toxoid is recommended in those whose vaccinations are not up to date and have a bite that punctures the skin.(ib. WHO2013) Tetanus immune globulin is indicated in people with more than 10 years since prior vaccination. Tetanus boosters (Td) should be given every ten years.

Bi-specific T-cell engager
▽ <665982> △ <2015-08-28> § <TOC|KW> ☆
[[(Fig.)Structure of a BiTE. VH: Heavy chain variable regions. VL: Light chain variable regions. Different specificity is marked with different colours and shapes. The arrows point from N- to C-terminus.]]
Bi-specific T-cell engagers (BiTEs) are a class of artificial bispecific monoclonal antibodies that are investigated for the use as anti-cancer drugs. They direct a host's immune system, more specifically the T cells' cytotoxic activity, against cancer cells. BiTE is a registered trademark of Micromet AG.([1]web: US Trademark registration no. 3,068,856, serial number 78/040,636. http://tarr.uspto.gov/servlet/tarr?regser=serial&entry=78040636 )
BiTEs are fusion proteins consisting of two single-chain variable fragments (scFvs) of different antibodies, or amino acid sequences from four different genes, on a single peptide chain of about 55 kilodaltons. One of the scFvs binds to T cells via the CD3 receptor, and the other to a tumor cell via a tumor specific molecule.(ib. "Oncology NEWS")([2]「BiTE-Antikörper: Durch Bispezifität T-Lymphozyten gegen Tumorzellen richten」『Journal Onkologie』第4号（German）、2008年。[ http://www.journalonko.de/aktuellview.php?id=1656 ])

■Mechanism of action
[[(Fig.)A BiTE linking a T cell to a tumor cell.]]
Like other bispecific antibodies, and unlike ordinary monoclonal antibodies, BiTEs form a link between T cells and tumor cells. This causes T cells to exert cytotoxic activity on tumor cells by producing proteins like perforin and granzymes, independently of the presence of MHC I or co-stimulatory molecules. These proteins enter tumor cells and initiate the cell's apoptosis.(ib. "Oncology NEWS")([3]web: BiTE Antibody Platform http://www.micromet.de/index.php?id=67 )
This action mimics physiological processes observed during T cell attacks against tumor cells.(ib. "Micromet")

□Further research
Utilizing the same technology, melanoma (with MCSP specific BiTEs) and acute myeloid leukemia (with CD33 specific BiTEs) can be targeted.([8]Kischel, R「Characterization in primates of MCSP- and CD33-specific human BiTE antibodies for treatment of Melanoma and AML」『Proc Am Assoc Cancer Res』第99巻、2008年。[ http://www.micromet.de/fileadmin/template/main/pdf/download/AACR_2008_MCSP_CD33_BiTE_Poster.pdf ]) Research in this area is currently ongoing. {{Album ratings| rev1 = Smash Hits| rev1score =([1]news: Smash Hits Album Reviews: Altered Imagaes - Bite )|rev2 = Allmusic|rev2score =([2][Allmusic review])}}Bite is the third and final studio album by the Scottish band Altered Images. It was released in 1983 and was produced by famed producers Mike Chapman and Tony Visconti. It features the UK top ten single "Don't Talk to Me About Love" as well as other singles: "Bring Me Closer", "Love to Stay" and "Change of Heart".

■Overview
This album followed the success of their previous two albums as well as a number of hit singles in the UK. For this album, the group recorded with producer Mike Chapman between October and December 1982 and then with Tony Visconti in March 1983.([3][Discogs - Bite production details http://www.discogs.com/Altered-Images-Bite/release/689374 ]) The resulting album was a change in direction from the group's earlier post punk route, becoming a more polished pure pop sound. This direction was controversial with the group's fanbase, and although the album peaked in the UK Top 20 (#16),([4][Chartstats - Bite UK chart details http://www.chartstats.com/albuminfo.php?id=7268 ]) it sold less than the group's previous two albums, both of which had been certified Silver by the BPI.([5][BPI - Certified awards - Altered Images http://www.bpi.co.uk/certifiedawards/search.aspx ])
The first single, "Don't Talk to Me About Love", reached UK #7, becoming one of the group's biggest hits, although further releases became less and less popular ("Bring Me Closer" UK #29, "Love to Stay" #46, "Change of Heart" #83).([6][Chartstats - Altered Images UK chart discography http://www.chartstats.com/artistinfo.php?id=3431 ]) Soon after the final single and the group completed a UK tour, they disbanded.
Originally released on vinyl album and cassette, the album was later re-issued on compact disc in 2004 with several bonus tracks as Bite...Plus. This version substituted a live version of "Another Lost Look", which had been featured originally on the B-side to "Love to Stay", and failed to include the original album version. Also, it included the single version of "Love to Stay" where originally an extended dance version of the track had appeared on the vinyl album, although the extended version does appear at the end of the CD issue.([7][Amazon - Bite...Plus http://www.amazon.co.uk/Bite-Plus-Altered-Images/dp/B00016W7PY ])

Steven Alan Hassan (born 1954) is an American licensed mental health counselor who has written extensively on the subject of cults.([2]web: Steven Hassan Verification - Psychology Today http://therapists.psychologytoday.com/rms/verification_personal.php?id=108149 ) He is the author of three books on the subject of destructive cults, and what he describes as their use of mind control, thought reform, and the psychology of influence in order to recruit and retain members.

■Background
Hassan became a member of the Unification Church (aka Moonies) in the 1970s, at the age of 19, while studying at Queens College. He describes what he terms as his "recruitment" in his first book, Combatting Cult Mind Control, asserting that this recruitment was the result of the unethical use of powerful psychological influence techniques by members of the Church.([5]Combatting Cult Mind Control, Steven Hassan, 1998, Ch. 1, ISBN 0-89281-243-5) He subsequently spent over two years recruiting and indoctrinating new members, as well as performing fundraising and campaigning duties, and ultimately rose to the rank of Assistant Director of the Unification Church at its National Headquarters. Animal bite 動物咬傷
▽ <415660> △ <2015-12-01> § <TOC|KW> ☆
[[(Fig.)USN Medic Louis Kost gets attacked by Bruno crop retouch]]
An animal bite is a wound received from the teeth of an animal, including humans. Animals may bite in self-defence, in an attempt to prey on food, and as part of normal interactions. Other bite attacks may be apparently unprovoked. Self-inflicted bites occur in some genetic illnesses, such as Lesch-Nyhan syndrome. Biting is an act that occurs when an animal uses its teeth to pierce another object, including food, flesh, and inanimate matter. A person bitten by an animal potentially carrying parvovirus or rabies virus should consult a physician immediately. Those who have been bitten by an animal may also develop bacterial infections of the bone called osteomyelitis which can become life-threatening if untreated, whether or not the animal has parvovirus or rabies virus.

■Signs and symptoms
Bite wounds can cause a number of signs and symptoms
[[Impala killed and skinned by leopard bites]]
・Generalized tissue damage due to tearing and scratching
・Serious hemorrhage if major blood vessels are pierced
・Infection by bacteria or other pathogens, including rabies
・Introduction of venom into the wound by venomous animals such as some snakes
・Introduction of other irritants into the wound, causing inflammation and itching

■Causes
[[(Fig.)A mosquito bite]]
Bites are usually classified by the type of creature causing the wound.Many different creatures are known to bite humans.

□Vertebrates
・Dog bites are commonplace, with children the most commonly bitten and the face the most common target.([1]web: Dog Bite Statistics http://www.dogbitelaw.com/PAGES/statistics.html ) About 4.7 million dog bites are reported annually in the United States.([2][Questions and Answers about Dog Bites http://www.hsus.org/pets/pet_care/dog_care/stay_dog_bite_free/questions_and_answers_about_dog_bites/ ][リンク切れ])
・Other companion animals, including cats, ferrets, and parrots, may bite humans.
・Wildlife may sometimes bite humans. The bites of various mammals such as bats, skunks, wolves, raccoons, etc. may transmit rabies, which is almost always fatal if left untreated.[citation needed]
Involuntary biting injuries due to closed-fist injuries from fists striking teeth (referred to as reverse bite injuries) are a common consequence of fist fights. These have been termed "fight bites". Injuries in which the knuckle joints or tendons of the hand are bitten into tend to be the most serious.
Teething infants are known to bite objects to relieve pressure on their growing teeth, and may inadvertently bite people's hands or arms while doing so. Young children may also bite people out of anger or misbehaviour, although this is usually corrected early in the child's life.
In spite of their name, love bites are not biting injuries (they involve bruising from sucking, and the skin is not broken), although actual biting injuries are sometimes seen as the result of fetishistic activities.

■Treatment
Initial treatment includes washing the bite wound.([3]web: Animal bites Fact sheet N°373 http://www.who.int/mediacentre/factsheets/fs373/en/ ) If there is a low risk of infection the wound may be sutured.(ib. WHO2013)

□Antibiotics
Antibiotics to prevent infection are recommended for dog and cat bites of the hand,([4]web: BestBets: Antibiotics in cat bites http://www.bestbets.org/bets/bet.php?id=1021 ) and human bites if they are more than superficial.([5]Oehler RL, Velez AP, Mizrachi M, Lamarche J, Gompf S「Bite-related and septic syndrome caused by cats and dogs」『Lancet Infect Dis』第9巻第7号、2009年。439-47頁。DOI[ http://dx.doi.org/10.1016/S1473-3099(09)70110-0 ]) They are also recommended in those who have poor immune function.(ib. WHO2013) Evidence for antibiotics to prevent infection in bites in other areas is not clear.([6]Medeiros I, Saconato H「Antibiotic prophylaxis for mammalian bites」『Cochrane Database Syst Rev』第2号、2001年。CD001738頁。DOI[ http://dx.doi.org/10.1002/14651858.CD001738 ])
The first choice is amoxicillin with clavulanic acid, and if the person is penicillin-allergic, doxycycline and metronidazole.(ib. "Oehler2009") The antistaphylococcal penicillins (e.g., cloxacillin, nafcillin, flucloxacillin) and the macrolides (e.g., erythromycin, clarithromycin) are not used for empirical therapy, because they do not cover Pasteurella species.(ib. "Oehler2009")

□Rabies
Animal bites, most commonly dogs and bats, transmit rabies to humans.([7]web: Rabies Fact Sheet N°99 http://www.who.int/mediacentre/factsheets/fs099/en/ ) Rabies from other animals is rare.(ib. WHO2015) If the animal is caught alive or dead with its head preserved, the head can be analyzed to detect the disease. Signs of rabies include foaming at the mouth, self-mutilation, growling, jerky behavior, and red eyes.
If the animal cannot be captured, preventative rabies treatment is recommended in many places. Certain places, such as Hawaii, Australia, and the United Kingdom, are known not to have native rabies. Treatment is generally available in North America and the Northern European states.

□Tetanus
Tetanus toxoid is recommended in those whose vaccinations are not up to date and have a bite that punctures the skin.(ib. WHO2013) Tetanus immune globulin is indicated in people with more than 10 years since prior vaccination. Tetanus boosters (Td) should be given every ten years.

Bi-specific T-cell engager
▽ <665982> △ <2015-08-28> § <TOC|KW> ☆
[[(Fig.)Structure of a BiTE. VH: Heavy chain variable regions. VL: Light chain variable regions. Different specificity is marked with different colours and shapes. The arrows point from N- to C-terminus.]]
Bi-specific T-cell engagers (BiTEs) are a class of artificial bispecific monoclonal antibodies that are investigated for the use as anti-cancer drugs. They direct a host's immune system, more specifically the T cells' cytotoxic activity, against cancer cells. BiTE is a registered trademark of Micromet AG.([1]web: US Trademark registration no. 3,068,856, serial number 78/040,636. http://tarr.uspto.gov/servlet/tarr?regser=serial&entry=78040636 )
BiTEs are fusion proteins consisting of two single-chain variable fragments (scFvs) of different antibodies, or amino acid sequences from four different genes, on a single peptide chain of about 55 kilodaltons. One of the scFvs binds to T cells via the CD3 receptor, and the other to a tumor cell via a tumor specific molecule.(ib. "Oncology NEWS")([2]「BiTE-Antikörper: Durch Bispezifität T-Lymphozyten gegen Tumorzellen richten」『Journal Onkologie』第4号（German）、2008年。[ http://www.journalonko.de/aktuellview.php?id=1656 ])

■Mechanism of action
[[(Fig.)A BiTE linking a T cell to a tumor cell.]]
Like other bispecific antibodies, and unlike ordinary monoclonal antibodies, BiTEs form a link between T cells and tumor cells. This causes T cells to exert cytotoxic activity on tumor cells by producing proteins like perforin and granzymes, independently of the presence of MHC I or co-stimulatory molecules. These proteins enter tumor cells and initiate the cell's apoptosis.(ib. "Oncology NEWS")([3]web: BiTE Antibody Platform http://www.micromet.de/index.php?id=67 )
This action mimics physiological processes observed during T cell attacks against tumor cells.(ib. "Micromet")

■Overview
This album followed the success of their previous two albums as well as a number of hit singles in the UK. Originally released on vinyl album and cassette, the album was later re-issued on compact disc in 2004 with several bonus tracks as Bite...Plus. This version substituted a live version of "Another Lost Look", which had been featured originally on the B-side to "Love to Stay", and failed to include the original album version. An animal bite is a wound received from the teeth of an animal, including humans. Animals may bite in self-defence, in an attempt to prey on food, and as part of normal interactions. Other bite attacks may be apparently unprovoked. Self-inflicted bites occur in some genetic illnesses, such as Lesch-Nyhan syndrome. Biting is an act that occurs when an animal uses its teeth to pierce another object, including food, flesh, and inanimate matter. A person bitten by an animal potentially carrying parvovirus or rabies virus should consult a physician immediately. Those who have been bitten by an animal may also develop bacterial infections of the bone called osteomyelitis which can become life-threatening if untreated, whether or not the animal has parvovirus or rabies virus.

■Signs and symptoms
Bite wounds can cause a number of signs and symptoms
[[Impala killed and skinned by leopard bites]]
・Generalized tissue damage due to tearing and scratching
・Serious hemorrhage if major blood vessels are pierced
・Infection by bacteria or other pathogens, including rabies
・Introduction of venom into the wound by venomous animals such as some snakes
・Introduction of other irritants into the wound, causing inflammation and itching

■Causes
[[(Fig.)A mosquito bite]]
Bites are usually classified by the type of creature causing the wound.Many different creatures are known to bite humans.

□Vertebrates
・Dog bites are commonplace, with children the most commonly bitten and the face the most common target.([1]web: Dog Bite Statistics http://www.dogbitelaw.com/PAGES/statistics.html ) About 4.7 million dog bites are reported annually in the United States.([2][Questions and Answers about Dog Bites http://www.hsus.org/pets/pet_care/dog_care/stay_dog_bite_free/questions_and_answers_about_dog_bites/ ][リンク切れ])
・Other companion animals, including cats, ferrets, and parrots, may bite humans.
・Wildlife may sometimes bite humans. The bites of various mammals such as bats, skunks, wolves, raccoons, etc. may transmit rabies, which is almost always fatal if left untreated.[citation needed]
Involuntary biting injuries due to closed-fist injuries from fists striking teeth (referred to as reverse bite injuries) are a common consequence of fist fights. These have been termed "fight bites". Injuries in which the knuckle joints or tendons of the hand are bitten into tend to be the most serious.
Teething infants are known to bite objects to relieve pressure on their growing teeth, and may inadvertently bite people's hands or arms while doing so. Young children may also bite people out of anger or misbehaviour, although this is usually corrected early in the child's life.
In spite of their name, love bites are not biting injuries (they involve bruising from sucking, and the skin is not broken), although actual biting injuries are sometimes seen as the result of fetishistic activities.

■Treatment
Initial treatment includes washing the bite wound.([3]web: Animal bites Fact sheet N°373 http://www.who.int/mediacentre/factsheets/fs373/en/ ) If there is a low risk of infection the wound may be sutured.(ib. WHO2013)

□Antibiotics
Antibiotics to prevent infection are recommended for dog and cat bites of the hand,([4]web: BestBets: Antibiotics in cat bites http://www.bestbets.org/bets/bet.php?id=1021 ) and human bites if they are more than superficial.([5]Oehler RL, Velez AP, Mizrachi M, Lamarche J, Gompf S「Bite-related and septic syndrome caused by cats and dogs」『Lancet Infect Dis』第9巻第7号、2009年。439-47頁。DOI[ http://dx.doi.org/10.1016/S1473-3099(09)70110-0 ]) They are also recommended in those who have poor immune function.(ib. WHO2013) Evidence for antibiotics to prevent infection in bites in other areas is not clear.([6]Medeiros I, Saconato H「Antibiotic prophylaxis for mammalian bites」『Cochrane Database Syst Rev』第2号、2001年。CD001738頁。DOI[ http://dx.doi.org/10.1002/14651858.CD001738 ])
The first choice is amoxicillin with clavulanic acid, and if the person is penicillin-allergic, doxycycline and metronidazole.(ib. "Oehler2009") The antistaphylococcal penicillins (e.g., cloxacillin, nafcillin, flucloxacillin) and the macrolides (e.g., erythromycin, clarithromycin) are not used for empirical therapy, because they do not cover Pasteurella species.(ib. "Oehler2009")

□Rabies
Animal bites, most commonly dogs and bats, transmit rabies to humans.([7]web: Rabies Fact Sheet N°99 http://www.who.int/mediacentre/factsheets/fs099/en/ ) Rabies from other animals is rare.(ib. WHO2015) If the animal is caught alive or dead with its head preserved, the head can be analyzed to detect the disease. Signs of rabies include foaming at the mouth, self-mutilation, growling, jerky behavior, and red eyes.
If the animal cannot be captured, preventative rabies treatment is recommended in many places. Certain places, such as Hawaii, Australia, and the United Kingdom, are known not to have native rabies. Treatment is generally available in North America and the Northern European states.

□Tetanus
Tetanus toxoid is recommended in those whose vaccinations are not up to date and have a bite that punctures the skin.(ib. WHO2013) Tetanus immune globulin is indicated in people with more than 10 years since prior vaccination. Tetanus boosters (Td) should be given every ten years.

Bi-specific T-cell engager
▽ <665982> △ <2015-08-28> § <TOC|KW> ☆
[[(Fig.)Structure of a BiTE. VH: Heavy chain variable regions. VL: Light chain variable regions. Different specificity is marked with different colours and shapes. The arrows point from N- to C-terminus.]]
Bi-specific T-cell engagers (BiTEs) are a class of artificial bispecific monoclonal antibodies that are investigated for the use as anti-cancer drugs. They direct a host's immune system, more specifically the T cells' cytotoxic activity, against cancer cells. BiTE is a registered trademark of Micromet AG.([1]web: US Trademark registration no. 3,068,856, serial number 78/040,636. http://tarr.uspto.gov/servlet/tarr?regser=serial&entry=78040636 )
BiTEs are fusion proteins consisting of two single-chain variable fragments (scFvs) of different antibodies, or amino acid sequences from four different genes, on a single peptide chain of about 55 kilodaltons. One of the scFvs binds to T cells via the CD3 receptor, and the other to a tumor cell via a tumor specific molecule.(ib. "Oncology NEWS")([2]「BiTE-Antikörper: Durch Bispezifität T-Lymphozyten gegen Tumorzellen richten」『Journal Onkologie』第4号（German）、2008年。[ http://www.journalonko.de/aktuellview.php?id=1656 ])

■Mechanism of action
[[(Fig.)A BiTE linking a T cell to a tumor cell.]]
Like other bispecific antibodies, and unlike ordinary monoclonal antibodies, BiTEs form a link between T cells and tumor cells. This causes T cells to exert cytotoxic activity on tumor cells by producing proteins like perforin and granzymes, independently of the presence of MHC I or co-stimulatory molecules. These proteins enter tumor cells and initiate the cell's apoptosis.(ib. "Oncology NEWS")([3]web: BiTE Antibody Platform http://www.micromet.de/index.php?id=67 )
This action mimics physiological processes observed during T cell attacks against tumor cells.(ib. "Micromet")

■Overview

Animal bite 動物咬傷
▽ <415660> △ <2015-12-01> § <TOC|KW> ☆
[[(Fig.)USN Medic Louis Kost gets attacked by Bruno crop retouch]]
An animal bite is a wound received from the teeth of an animal, including humans. Animals may bite in self-defence, in an attempt to prey on food, and as part of normal interactions. Other bite attacks may be apparently unprovoked. Self-inflicted bites occur in some genetic illnesses, such as Lesch-Nyhan syndrome. Biting is an act that occurs when an animal uses its teeth to pierce another object, including food, flesh, and inanimate matter. A person bitten by an animal potentially carrying parvovirus or rabies virus should consult a physician immediately. Those who have been bitten by an animal may also develop bacterial infections of the bone called osteomyelitis which can become life-threatening if untreated, whether or not the animal has parvovirus or rabies virus.

■Signs and symptoms
Bite wounds can cause a number of signs and symptoms
[[Impala killed and skinned by leopard bites]]
・Generalized tissue damage due to tearing and scratching
・Serious hemorrhage if major blood vessels are pierced
・Infection by bacteria or other pathogens, including rabies
・Introduction of venom into the wound by venomous animals such as some snakes
・Introduction of other irritants into the wound, causing inflammation and itching

■Causes
[[(Fig.)A mosquito bite]]
Bites are usually classified by the type of creature causing the wound.Many different creatures are known to bite humans.

□Vertebrates
・Dog bites are commonplace, with children the most commonly bitten and the face the most common target.([1]web: Dog Bite Statistics http://www.dogbitelaw.com/PAGES/statistics.html ) About 4.7 million dog bites are reported annually in the United States.([2][Questions and Answers about Dog Bites http://www.hsus.org/pets/pet_care/dog_care/stay_dog_bite_free/questions_and_answers_about_dog_bites/ ][リンク切れ])
・Other companion animals, including cats, ferrets, and parrots, may bite humans.
・Wildlife may sometimes bite humans. The bites of various mammals such as bats, skunks, wolves, raccoons, etc. may transmit rabies, which is almost always fatal if left untreated.[citation needed]
Involuntary biting injuries due to closed-fist injuries from fists striking teeth (referred to as reverse bite injuries) are a common consequence of fist fights. These have been termed "fight bites". Injuries in which the knuckle joints or tendons of the hand are bitten into tend to be the most serious.
Teething infants are known to bite objects to relieve pressure on their growing teeth, and may inadvertently bite people's hands or arms while doing so. Young children may also bite people out of anger or misbehaviour, although this is usually corrected early in the child's life.
In spite of their name, love bites are not biting injuries (they involve bruising from sucking, and the skin is not broken), although actual biting injuries are sometimes seen as the result of fetishistic activities.

■Treatment
Initial treatment includes washing the bite wound.([3]web: Animal bites Fact sheet N°373 http://www.who.int/mediacentre/factsheets/fs373/en/ ) If there is a low risk of infection the wound may be sutured.(ib. WHO2013)

□Antibiotics
Antibiotics to prevent infection are recommended for dog and cat bites of the hand,([4]web: BestBets: Antibiotics in cat bites http://www.bestbets.org/bets/bet.php?id=1021 ) and human bites if they are more than superficial.([5]Oehler RL, Velez AP, Mizrachi M, Lamarche J, Gompf S「Bite-related and septic syndrome caused by cats and dogs」『Lancet Infect Dis』第9巻第7号、2009年。439-47頁。DOI[ http://dx.doi.org/10.1016/S1473-3099(09)70110-0 ]) They are also recommended in those who have poor immune function.(ib. WHO2013) Evidence for antibiotics to prevent infection in bites in other areas is not clear.([6]Medeiros I, Saconato H「Antibiotic prophylaxis for mammalian bites」『Cochrane Database Syst Rev』第2号、2001年。CD001738頁。DOI[ http://dx.doi.org/10.1002/14651858.CD001738 ])
The first choice is amoxicillin with clavulanic acid, and if the person is penicillin-allergic, doxycycline and metronidazole.(ib. "Oehler2009") The antistaphylococcal penicillins (e.g., cloxacillin, nafcillin, flucloxacillin) and the macrolides (e.g., erythromycin, clarithromycin) are not used for empirical therapy, because they do not cover Pasteurella species.(ib. "Oehler2009")

□Rabies
Animal bites, most commonly dogs and bats, transmit rabies to humans.([7]web: Rabies Fact Sheet N°99 http://www.who.int/mediacentre/factsheets/fs099/en/ ) Rabies from other animals is rare.(ib. WHO2015) If the animal is caught alive or dead with its head preserved, the head can be analyzed to detect the disease. Signs of rabies include foaming at the mouth, self-mutilation, growling, jerky behavior, and red eyes.
If the animal cannot be captured, preventative rabies treatment is recommended in many places. Certain places, such as Hawaii, Australia, and the United Kingdom, are known not to have native rabies. Treatment is generally available in North America and the Northern European states.

□Tetanus
Tetanus toxoid is recommended in those whose vaccinations are not up to date and have a bite that punctures the skin.(ib. WHO2013) Tetanus immune globulin is indicated in people with more than 10 years since prior vaccination. Tetanus boosters (Td) should be given every ten years.

Bi-specific T-cell engager
▽ <665982> △ <2015-08-28> § <TOC|KW> ☆
[[(Fig.)Structure of a BiTE. VH: Heavy chain variable regions. VL: Light chain variable regions. Different specificity is marked with different colours and shapes. The arrows point from N- to C-terminus.]]
Bi-specific T-cell engagers (BiTEs) are a class of artificial bispecific monoclonal antibodies that are investigated for the use as anti-cancer drugs. They direct a host's immune system, more specifically the T cells' cytotoxic activity, against cancer cells. BiTE is a registered trademark of Micromet AG.([1]web: US Trademark registration no. 3,068,856, serial number 78/040,636. http://tarr.uspto.gov/servlet/tarr?regser=serial&entry=78040636 )
BiTEs are fusion proteins consisting of two single-chain variable fragments (scFvs) of different antibodies, or amino acid sequences from four different genes, on a single peptide chain of about 55 kilodaltons. One of the scFvs binds to T cells via the CD3 receptor, and the other to a tumor cell via a tumor specific molecule.(ib. "Oncology NEWS")([2]「BiTE-Antikörper: Durch Bispezifität T-Lymphozyten gegen Tumorzellen richten」『Journal Onkologie』第4号（German）、2008年。[ http://www.journalonko.de/aktuellview.php?id=1656 ])

■Mechanism of action
[[(Fig.)A BiTE linking a T cell to a tumor cell.]]
Like other bispecific antibodies, and unlike ordinary monoclonal antibodies, BiTEs form a link between T cells and tumor cells. This causes T cells to exert cytotoxic activity on tumor cells by producing proteins like perforin and granzymes, independently of the presence of MHC I or co-stimulatory molecules. These proteins enter tumor cells and initiate the cell's apoptosis.(ib. "Oncology NEWS")([3]web: BiTE Antibody Platform http://www.micromet.de/index.php?id=67 )
This action mimics physiological processes observed during T cell attacks against tumor cells.(ib. "Micromet")

{{Album ratings| rev1 = Smash Hits| rev1score =([1]news: Smash Hits Album Reviews: Altered Imagaes - Bite )|rev2 = Allmusic|rev2score =([2][Allmusic review])}}Bite is the third and final studio album by the Scottish band Altered Images. It was released in 1983 and was produced by famed producers Mike Chapman and Tony Visconti. It features the UK top ten single "Don't Talk to Me About Love" as well as other singles: "Bring Me Closer", "Love to Stay" and "Change of Heart".

■Overview
This album followed the success of their previous two albums as well as a number of hit singles in the UK. For this album, the group recorded with producer Mike Chapman between October and December 1982 and then with Tony Visconti in March 1983.([3][Discogs - Bite production details http://www.discogs.com/Altered-Images-Bite/release/689374 ]) The resulting album was a change in direction from the group's earlier post punk route, becoming a more polished pure pop sound. This direction was controversial with the group's fanbase, and although the album peaked in the UK Top 20 (#16),([4][Chartstats - Bite UK chart details http://www.chartstats.com/albuminfo.php?id=7268 ]) it sold less than the group's previous two albums, both of which had been certified Silver by the BPI.([5][BPI - Certified awards - Altered Images http://www.bpi.co.uk/certifiedawards/search.aspx ])
The first single, "Don't Talk to Me About Love", reached UK #7, becoming one of the group's biggest hits, although further releases became less and less popular ("Bring Me Closer" UK #29, "Love to Stay" #46, "Change of Heart" #83).([6][Chartstats - Altered Images UK chart discography http://www.chartstats.com/artistinfo.php?id=3431 ]) Soon after the final single and the group completed a UK tour, they disbanded.
Originally released on vinyl album and cassette, the album was later re-issued on compact disc in 2004 with several bonus tracks as Bite...Plus. This version substituted a live version of "Another Lost Look", which had been featured originally on the B-side to "Love to Stay", and failed to include the original album version. Also, it included the single version of "Love to Stay" where originally an extended dance version of the track had appeared on the vinyl album, although the extended version does appear at the end of the CD issue.([7][Amazon - Bite...Plus http://www.amazon.co.uk/Bite-Plus-Altered-Images/dp/B00016W7PY ])

Steven Alan Hassan (born 1954) is an American licensed mental health counselor who has written extensively on the subject of cults.([2]web: Steven Hassan Verification - Psychology Today http://therapists.psychologytoday.com/rms/verification_personal.php?id=108149 ) He is the author of three books on the subject of destructive cults, and what he describes as their use of mind control, thought reform, and the psychology of influence in order to recruit and retain members.

March 17, 1992.) developing in 1999 what he describes as his own non-coercive methods for helping members of alleged cults to leave their groups.

■Background
Hassan became a member of the Unification Church (aka Moonies) in the 1970s, at the age of 19, while studying at Queens College. He describes what he terms as his "recruitment" in his first book, Combatting Cult Mind Control, asserting that this recruitment was the result of the unethical use of powerful psychological influence techniques by members of the Church.([5]Combatting Cult Mind Control, Steven Hassan, 1998, Ch. 1, ISBN 0-89281-243-5) He subsequently spent over two years recruiting and indoctrinating new members, as well as performing fundraising and campaigning duties, and ultimately rose to the rank of Assistant Director of the Unification Church at its National Headquarters. In that capacity he met personally with Sun Myung Moon.([6][Biography http://freedomofmind.com/Media/biography.php ] of Steven Hassan, Freedom of Mind Center)
Hassan has given an account of his leaving the Unification Church in his 1998 book Combatting Cult Mind Control and on his personal website.([7]Combatting Cult Mind Control, Steven Hassan, 1998, Ch. 2, ISBN 0-89281-243-5)
